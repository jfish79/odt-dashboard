About this database

This database tracks same-day, app- or phone-bookable public on-demand transit deployments across the United States — commonly called microtransit or demand-response transit. It is a research inventory, not an official government registry. Data is compiled from agency websites, press releases, state DOT studies, and the Federal Transit Administration. All coverage areas and fleet figures are approximate.

Field definitions
Geographic Context
Characterizes the land-use environment of the primary service area. Assigned by the research team based on Census urbanized area classification and service zone description.
Urban
Service area is primarily within a city core or densely developed urban district. Typically characterized by Census-defined urbanized areas with >50,000 population, walkable street grids, and connections to rail or frequent bus.
Examples: GoGastonia (NC), Via Jersey City (NJ), MARTA Reach (GA), Metro Micro zones (CA)
Suburban
Service area is primarily within a suburban municipality or outer metropolitan ring. Typically lower density than urban, auto-oriented land use, often first/last-mile to commuter rail or park-and-ride. May include Census "urban cluster" areas (2,500–50,000 population).
Examples: RTD FlexRide zones (CO), Metro Flex (WA), Ride Gwinnett (GA), CATS Micro (NC)
Rural
Service area is primarily in a rural county or low-density region outside any Census urbanized area. Trips are typically longer, vehicles are fewer, and connections to fixed-route transit are limited or absent. Often demand-response in nature.
Examples: YVEDDI GOTransit (NC), Clallam Transit Interlink (WA), Bay Transit Express (VA), GoWake SmartRide NE (NC)
Mixed
Service area meaningfully crosses multiple context types — for example, a military installation with adjacent civilian communities, or a zone bridging a city core and outlying suburban areas.
Examples: Cavazos Connector (TX — military/urban), The HOP (TX — multi-city urban/rural)
System Type
Distinguishes the scheduling model of the service.
Microtransit
Rides are booked same-day, with no fixed schedule or route. The algorithm dynamically routes vehicles in real time to serve multiple passengers. Pickup is typically within 15–30 minutes of request. Synonymous with "on-demand transit" in this database.
Demand-Response
Rides are booked in advance (typically the prior day or further out). Vehicles are dynamically routed but scheduled rather than immediately dispatched. Common in rural settings. Open to the general public. Flagged with "Advance" scheduling window in this database.
Paratransit
Eligibility-restricted demand-response service, typically limited to seniors, people with disabilities, or ADA-certified riders. Advance booking required. Added during the June 2026 supplemental data integration to distinguish these from general-public demand-response services.
Status / Maturity
Describes the operational lifecycle stage of the service.
ActiveCurrently operating with stable funding and no announced end date.
Active – ExpandingActively operating and adding zones, fleet, or service hours.
Active – NewRecently launched (approximately within the last 6 months of the database's last update).
Active – Post PilotGraduated from a time-limited pilot into permanent or ongoing funded service.
PilotActively operating under a time-limited or experimental funding authorization.
Pilot – EndedPilot has concluded. Permanent status (continuation, suspension, or transition) unknown or pending announcement.
Scheduling Window
How far in advance rides must be booked.
Same-dayRides can be booked and received within the same operating day, typically within minutes to an hour of request. The defining characteristic of true on-demand / microtransit.
AdvanceRides must be booked at least one day before travel. Common in demand-response systems and rural programs.
FlexibleSystem supports both same-day and advance booking, often with different lead times for different trip types or zones.
Technology Vendor
The software platform provider powering the dispatch, routing, and rider app. Distinct from the operator (who runs the vehicles) and the funding agency (who pays for the service). Many agencies procure the transit management contract and the technology platform separately.
ViaNew York-based TransitTech company, the dominant US market platform (~39 deployments in this database). Powers both app and operations management. Agencies often brand the service independently of Via's name.
SpareVancouver-based platform focused on transit agency integration, paratransit, and multi-modal trip planning. Used by DART GoLink, CATS Micro, Metro Transit Micro, and others.
RideCoToronto-based on-demand transit software. Used by LA Metro Micro, Ride Gwinnett, OmniRide (Omnitrans), and others.
FreebeeMiami-based operator-and-software provider. Unlike pure software vendors, Freebee also operates the vehicles. Predominantly deployed in Florida municipalities.
The Routing Company (TRC/Pingo)Boston-based platform. Consumer app is branded "Pingo." Used by Clallam Transit Interlink, CDTA FLEX+, FAST Connect.
Not specifiedThe technology vendor is unknown, the agency uses a proprietary system, or the agency has not publicly disclosed their software provider.
Data caveats

Fleet figures are sourced from press releases and agency reports and may not reflect current operations. Service hours and fares change frequently — verify with the agency before travel planning. Latitude/longitude pins represent approximate service area centers, not coverage boundaries. Systems marked "Not specified" have data that is not publicly available or not yet researched.

Data quality concerns

The June 2026 update added 120 systems from supplemental CA/CO lists. Several data quality issues were identified during integration and are documented below. The full log (including 83 individual paratransit reclassifications) is in the Concerns tab of the companion spreadsheet.

Vendor errors (verified)
Five systems in the supplemental lists had incorrect technology vendors. All were independently verified against vendor press releases and agency sources. The existing database values are correct; the supplemental values were discarded during integration.

The supplemental CA list labels 20 entries as "Via." Given that 4 of 4 checked were wrong, the entire vendor column for supplemental entries should be treated as unverified.

Paratransit reclassification
83 systems from the supplemental lists were reclassified from "Demand-Response" to "Paratransit" based on eligibility restrictions found in their names or notes (ADA, senior, disabled, age cutoffs). These are traditional eligibility-restricted dial-a-ride services, not general-public on-demand transit. Each reclassification is individually logged in the spreadsheet's Concerns tab. Filter by System Type = Paratransit and Source = Supplemental CA/CO list to isolate these entries.
Status change: OC Flex
OC Flex (Orange County, CA) was discontinued effective July 1, 2025 per OCTA's website. The supplemental list had status "Active." This should be updated to "Ended" or removed.
Deduplication
Two pre-existing duplicate pairs were resolved: CATS Micro (NC) had two entries with 8 differing fields — the more detailed entry was kept. Cupertino Microtransit and SV Hopper (CA) were the same Via-operated service listed twice — the richer SV Hopper entry was kept. Additionally, 12 systems that appeared in both the existing database and the supplemental lists were skipped (existing entries retained, since supplemental vendors were unreliable).
